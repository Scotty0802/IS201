About the Event

The Cooper's Hill Cheese-Rolling and Wake is an annual event held on the Spring Bank Holiday at Cooper's Hill, near Gloucester, England. Participants race down a 200-yard hill, chasing a wheel of Double Gloucester cheese, reaching speeds of up to 70 mph. The event draws competitors and spectators from all over the world.

History

Cheese rolling is believed to have pagan origins, linked to fertility rites and celebrating the end of winter. The first recorded mention of the event dates back to 1826, but it is thought to be over 600 years old. Over the years, it has evolved, with occasional cancellations due to safety concerns or global events like the COVID-19 pandemic.

Injuries are a common part of the tradition. In 1993, 15 people were injured during a single race. Despite safety concerns, the event continues to thrive as an unofficial tradition, beloved by locals and adventurers worldwide.

How to Participate

Anyone can join the race! Simply show up on the day of the event. Competitors race down the steep hill after a 7–9-pound round of Double Gloucester cheese. The first to cross the finish line wins the cheese.

Pro Tips:

  • Wear sturdy shoes with good grip.
  • Expect to tumble – staying upright is rare!
  • Be prepared for mud, bruises, and possible injuries.

Fun Facts

  1. Global Attraction:
    • Winners have come from Australia, Belgium, Canada, and the United States.
    • Competitors often describe it as “organized chaos.”
  2. Cheese Details:
    • The cheese is traditionally made from the milk of Gloucester cows.
    • It is aged for six months and has a rich, buttery flavor.
